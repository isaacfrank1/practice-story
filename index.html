Introduction

This is a data story about which Hartford corridors have the most crashes, by type:

As traffic related fatalities in Hartford are rising, mitigation efforts are needed more than ever. Pedestrian fatalities are at the highest rate in over three decades(Hartford Courant, 2022), and helping determine which corridors (roads, streets, avenues, etc) need the most attention is the start of change.

As seen in Figure 1, Main St. in Hartford has the most crashes out of any street. From the initial data gathering, Main Street also had the highest number of crash related injuries in each category from 2018-2022, with 1,153 individual incidents and 387 injuries.

Figure 1: This chart shows that Main St. has the highest number of crashes and injuries in every category.

But is Main St. the most dangerous?

To answer that question we need to put our data into context.

Right off the bat you may notice that the name “Main Street” denotes its centrality in Hartford, likely with high volumes of traffic and activity. In this regard we note the importance for normalizing our data.

As seen in Figure 2, we can normalize our data to find which street yields the most injuries by percentage of crashes:

Figure 2: This chart shows that Main St. is not an outlier in terms of injury type, and may not be the most dangerous corridor in Hartford.

Normalizing by Length

Main St. has the most crashes, but it is also the longest corridor...

As shown in Figure 3, the raw data shows Main St. looking like the most dangerous corridor. But is this accurate?

Figure 3: This chart shows that Main St. has far and away the most crashes out of Hartford corridors.

In order to compare corridors more accurately, we must put them on a level playing field.

Within our data, we divided every corridor's number of crashes by its total length (in meters) and charted them, as shown in Figure 4.

Figure 4: This chart shows that other streets not presented as dangerous as Main St. have more crashes per meter.
